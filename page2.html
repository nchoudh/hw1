Nadim Choudhury, MD

Physician and Business School Student

Summary

Dr. Nadim Choudhury is an MBA candidate at Kellogg. He is passionate about working at the intersection of medicine and business to improve healthcare delivery. Nadim graduated from the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai in 2016 where he spent his time outside of the classroom as a leader at the student-run free clinic in East Harlem and helping lead a surgical mission trip to Mongolia with the Virtue Foundation. He was published for research that he helped conduct in endocrinology and ophthalmology. At Kellogg, he has focused on improving the Health Care Club where he helped spearhead an effort to improve the HEMA curriculum. He is interning this summer at Edwards LifeSciences where he is working on a platform that helps better diagnose and appropriately treat patients with valvular heart disease. Before medical school, Nadim graduated Phi Beta Kappa from the University of Florida with a degree in the Neurobiological Sciences and a minor in Education.
